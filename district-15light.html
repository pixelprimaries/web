DISTRICT 15

Illinois's 15th Congressional District is a congressional district in the U.S. state of Illinois. The district covers eastern and southeastern portions of the state, including parts of Champaign, Coles, Vermilion, Effingham, Crawford, Edgar, and Lawrence counties. Key cities within the district include Charleston, Danville, Effingham, Mattoon, and Paris.

Historically, the 15th District has undergone significant boundary changes due to redistricting. For decades, it has been solidly Republican, reflecting the conservative political character of eastern and southeastern Illinois. The district primarily comprises rural agricultural communities, small towns, and regional commercial hubs, with agriculture and manufacturing forming the economic base. Republican Mary Miller currently represents the district, having been elected in 2020. Miller is known for conservative positions, focusing heavily on agriculture policy, rural development, and cultural issues aligned with the district's conservative electorate. Prior to Miller, Republican John Shimkus represented the district for many years, retiring in 2020 after serving since 1997. The district has a Cook Partisan Voting Index score of R+22, indicating a strong Republican lean. Demographically, the population is predominantly White, with small Black, Hispanic, and Asian populations concentrated mostly in the larger towns. The median household income is below the national average, consistent with the rural and small-town economic characteristics of the area.
