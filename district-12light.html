DISTRICT 12

Illinois's 12th Congressional District is a congressional district in the U.S. state of Illinois. The district covers the southern region of Illinois, including parts of Madison and St. Clair counties, as well as Alexander, Franklin, Hamilton, Jackson, Jefferson, Monroe, Perry, Pulaski, Randolph, Union, and Williamson counties. Notable cities within the district include Belleville, Carbondale, East St. Louis, Marion, and O'Fallon.

Historically, the 12th District has undergone various boundary adjustments due to redistricting, reflecting population shifts and political realignments in southern Illinois. Traditionally, the district was a Democratic stronghold, represented by Democrats such as Jerry Costello, who held the seat from 1988 until his retirement in 2013. Following Costello's retirement, the district became increasingly Republican-leaning, mirroring broader political trends in rural and southern Illinois. In 2014, Republican Mike Bost won the seat, marking a significant political shift. Bost, a former state legislator, has since been re-elected multiple times, focusing primarily on agriculture, infrastructure, veterans' affairs, and conservative policy positions aligned with the district's predominantly rural and conservative electorate. The district has a Cook Partisan Voting Index score of R+24, indicating a strong Republican lean. The population is predominantly White, with smaller Black, Hispanic, and Asian communities. Economically, the district is diverse, combining agricultural areas, coal-mining regions, manufacturing hubs, and educational centers such as Southern Illinois University in Carbondale. The median household income is below the national average, reflecting economic challenges common to rural and industrial communities.
