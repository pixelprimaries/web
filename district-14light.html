DISTRICT 14

Illinois's 14th Congressional District is a congressional district in the U.S. state of Illinois. The district covers parts of northern Illinois, including portions of DeKalb, Kane, Kendall, LaSalle, and Will counties. Major communities within the district include Aurora, Batavia, Geneva, St. Charles, Oswego, Yorkville, and Sycamore.

Historically, the 14th District was a reliably Republican district, represented for two decades by former Speaker of the House Dennis Hastert. Following Hastert's resignation in 2007, Democrat Bill Foster briefly held the seat until losing it to Republican Randy Hultgren in 2010. Hultgren retained the seat until 2018 when Democrat Lauren Underwood defeated him, marking a significant political shift in the district. Underwood, a nurse and public health expert, has focused her legislative efforts primarily on healthcare policy, veterans' affairs, and education. The district has a Cook Partisan Voting Index score of D+4, indicating a slight Democratic lean. Demographically, the district's population is predominantly White, with significant Hispanic, Asian, and Black communities. The area encompasses both suburban and rural regions, blending agricultural areas with growing suburban communities, especially around the Fox River Valley. Median household income in the district is above the national average, reflecting a mix of middle-class and affluent communities.
