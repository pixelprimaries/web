DISTRICT 17

Illinois's 17th Congressional District is a congressional district in the U.S. state of Illinois. The district covers northwestern and west-central parts of the state, including portions of Rock Island, Peoria, Knox, Whiteside, Henry, Mercer, Warren, Fulton, and Winnebago counties. Major cities within the district include Rock Island, Moline, East Moline, Galesburg, Peoria, and Sterling.

Historically, the 17th District has experienced several boundary changes due to redistricting. Traditionally, the district was represented by moderate Democrats, notably Lane Evans, who served from 1983 to 2007. Following Evans's retirement, Democrat Phil Hare represented the district until 2011, when Republican Bobby Schilling won the seat during the 2010 midterms. Democrat Cheri Bustos reclaimed the seat in 2012 and has represented the district since, maintaining a focus on agricultural policy, infrastructure improvements, economic development, and veterans' affairs. The district has a Cook Partisan Voting Index score of D+2, indicating a slight Democratic lean but making it consistently competitive. The population is predominantly White, with smaller Black, Hispanic, and Asian communities primarily concentrated in urban centers like Peoria and Rock Island. Economically, the district combines agricultural communities, manufacturing hubs, and river towns along the Mississippi and Illinois rivers. Median household income is below the national average, reflecting challenges common to both rural and industrial areas.
