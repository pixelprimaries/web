DISTRICT 9

Illinois's 9th Congressional District is a congressional district in the U.S. state of Illinois. The district covers parts of Cook, Lake, and McHenry counties, including areas such as Evanston, Skokie, Arlington Heights, Des Plaines, Park Ridge, and portions of Chicago's North Side.

The district has a long history of political engagement and demographic diversity. Over the years, redistricting has adjusted its boundaries to reflect population shifts, but it has remained centered around Chicago’s northern suburbs. The district includes both densely populated urban neighborhoods and suburban communities, contributing to its economic and social diversity. Since 1999, the district has been represented by Democrat Jan Schakowsky. It has been a Democratic stronghold for decades, consistently electing progressive candidates. The district has a strong liberal voting record, with Democratic candidates winning by significant margins in recent elections. Issues such as healthcare, environmental policy, and social justice are key concerns for many residents. The district has a Cook Partisan Voting Index score of D+20, indicating a strong Democratic lean. Its population is diverse, with a mix of White, Asian, Hispanic, and Black communities. The area includes both affluent neighborhoods and working-class communities, reflecting a range of economic conditions. The median household income is above the national average, and education levels are generally high, with a significant number of residents holding college degrees.
